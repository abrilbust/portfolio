VAW: Global Health Alliances
As the Co-Vice President of Education and Training for Penn's Chapter of VAW: Global Health Alliances, I helped launch and lead the chapter’s first medical internship program in Guatemala, focusing on expanding access to healthcare in underserved rural communities. I designed and facilitated a comprehensive pre-departure curriculum covering clinical skills, basic Spanish, and cultural competency to prepare over 10 student volunteers for international medical service. I also led fundraising and vendor partnership efforts that boosted program recruitment by 30% and donor contributions by 25%. During our time abroad, I worked across various clinical stations, including intake, diagnosis, and pharmacy, and collaborated with local healthcare professionals to deliver mobile primary care clinics, directly treating over 500 patients. This immersive experience not only deepened my understanding of global health inequities but also shaped my commitment to financially sustainable, community-driven health interventions. One eye-opening conversation with a local nurse left a lasting impact on me. She explained that many people, mostly children, contract parasites from the unfiltered water or suffer from illnesses that may be fatal without proper treatment every day. Their barriers to healthcare include the lack of education in understanding general hygiene, unaffordable precautionary measures (i.e. a water filter), and prohibitive medical fees. So, I asked, “What happens then?” Her response was stark and heartbreaking: “They die.” This answer stripped me of words. The nature of Guatemala’s healthcare crisis, exacerbated by issues like corruption, needs financially viable and innovative solutions. Bridging the gaps in healthcare accessibility, I was inspired to help revive the Guatemala Health Initiative at Penn, uniting students around health policy education and fundraising for Hospitalito Atitlán. For more information about VAW: Global Health Alliances, please visit their website.
