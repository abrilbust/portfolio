My Research at the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs

During my internship at the U.S. Department od Veterans Affairs Healthcare System, I contributed to an immunological research project investigating regulatory T cells as a potential therapeutic strategy from Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD). My role involved preparing and analyzing cell samples using tissue-to-single-cell suspension, density gradient centrifugation, and flow cytometry, which were techniques essential to support over 20 reproducible experiments. I collaborated closely with a multidisciplinary team to standarize protocols, troubleshoot experimental variability, and interpret data that could inform future targeted therapies. This hands-on experience deepened my understanding of translational research and culminated in an oral and poster presentation at the Michigan Health Sciences Research Symposium, where I shared our findings with the broader scientific community.
