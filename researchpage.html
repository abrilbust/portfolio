My Research at the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs

As a research intern at the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs in Ann Arbor, MI, I was given the opportuniy to contribute to groundbreaking research on regulatory T cell (Treg) subsets, a subpopulation of CD4+ T cells. Exploring their therapeutic benefit, we explored their role as a potential therapy for Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD). My work included measuring RNA expression, protein expression, and conducting chemotaxis experiments to evaluate treg subset functionality. I became more experienced in experimental design, data analysis, and research techniques including tissue to single cell suspension, cell isolation, gradient centrifugation, and flow cytometry.
